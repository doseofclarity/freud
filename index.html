Freud's Psychosexual Theory

Stages of Development | Nursing Application | Adele Pillitteri, 7e

Overview & Key Concepts

Sigmund Freud (1856–1939) proposed the first theory of personality development, emphasizing unconscious drives and psychosexual stages. Personality is formed through resolution of conflicts at each stage. Key structures: Id (pleasure principle), Ego (reality principle), Superego (morality).

🔍 Key Terms
Libido – psychic energy; Fixation – arrested development due to overindulgence or frustration; Regression – returning to earlier stage under stress.
📊 Mnemonic (Stages):
"O-A-PHA-LA-GE" – Oral, Anal, Phallic, Latency, Genital.
Psychosexual Development Timeline

Oral Stage (Birth – 1 year)

Pleasure zone: Mouth (sucking, biting, swallowing). Task: Weaning from breast/bottle. Fixation behaviors: Overindulgence → oral receptive (smoking, overeating, gullibility); Frustration → oral aggressive (sarcasm, hostility).

👩‍⚕️ Nursing Implications: Provide appropriate oral stimulation (pacifier, teething toys). Avoid rigid weaning. Support breastfeeding or bottle feeding with comfort measures.

Anal Stage (1–3 years)

Pleasure zone: Anus (retention and expulsion of feces). Task: Toilet training. Fixation: Anal retentive (orderly, stubborn, controlling) or anal expulsive (messy, disorganized, aggressive).

👩‍⚕️ Nursing Implications: Encourage gradual, positive toilet training; avoid shaming; praise success; allow toddler autonomy.

Phallic Stage (3–6 years)

Pleasure zone: Genitals. Tasks: Oedipus complex (boys) / Electra complex (girls); identification with same‑sex parent. Fixation: Sexual deviance, gender identity issues, vanity.

👩‍⚕️ Nursing Implications: Support gender role exploration, answer questions about anatomy honestly, avoid making child feel guilty about masturbation.

Latency Stage (6–12 years)

Pleasure zone: Dormant sexual feelings. Focus: Socialization, intellectual skills, peer relationships. Fixation: Difficulty forming intimate relationships in adulthood.

👩‍⚕️ Nursing Implications: Encourage same‑sex friendships, hobbies, school activities. Support cognitive and social development.

Genital Stage (12+ years)

Pleasure zone: Genitals (mature sexuality). Focus: Establishing intimate relationships, career, family. Healthy outcome: Ability to love and work.

👩‍⚕️ Nursing Implications: Provide sex education, promote healthy relationships, discuss contraception and STI prevention, support identity formation.

Criticisms & Nursing Implications

⚠️ Criticisms
Based on mentally ill patients, not general population; overemphasis on sexuality; lack of scientific evidence; gender bias; limited usefulness for health promotion.
✅ Contributions
First comprehensive personality theory; highlighted importance of early childhood experiences; introduced concepts of unconscious and defense mechanisms.
👩‍⚕️ Nursing Application: Use Freud's stages to understand behaviors, but do not rely solely on theory. Incorporate with other developmental theories for holistic care.

Flashcards & Mnemonics

Q: What is the pleasure source in the Oral stage?
Mouth (sucking, biting, swallowing)
Q: What fixation results from harsh toilet training?
Anal retentive (orderly, stubborn, controlling)
Q: What is the Oedipus complex?
Boy's unconscious desire for mother and rivalry with father
Q: Which stage has dormant sexual energy?
Latency stage (6–12 years)
Q: Failure to resolve a stage leads to?
Fixation (remaining stuck in that stage)
Q: Electra complex refers to?
Girl's unconscious desire for father and rivalry with mother
